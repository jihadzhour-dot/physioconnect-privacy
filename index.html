Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 19, 2026

PhysioConnect ("we", "our", or "us") operates the PhysioConnect mobile and desktop application (the "App"). This page explains what information we collect, why we collect it, and how we protect it. By using the App you agree to the practices described here.

1. Information We Collect

1a. Information you provide directly

  • Account information – name, email address, and password when you register.
  • Health and clinical data – physiotherapy session notes (SOAP notes), appointment records, diagnosis information, treatment plans, billing records, and session statistics entered by doctors or patients. This is classified as sensitive health data and is subject to the strongest protections described in Section 5.
  • Profile information – profile photo, phone number, date of birth, clinic name, and specialisation (for healthcare providers).
  • Expense and income records – financial data entered by doctors for their own clinic management.

1b. Information collected automatically

  • Location – approximate or precise device location, collected only when you use the "Find a Doctor" or "Update My Location" features and only with your explicit permission. We do not track your location in the background.
  • Device push token – a Firebase Cloud Messaging (FCM) token used solely to deliver push notifications about your appointments and clinical updates. It is stored server-side and never shared with third parties.
  • Usage and error data – basic server-side logs (Supabase) to diagnose errors. No third-party analytics SDKs are embedded in the App.

2. How We Use Your Information

  • Provide, operate, and maintain the App and its features.
  • Match patients with their assigned physiotherapists and share relevant clinical notes between them.
  • Send appointment reminders and clinical update notifications.
  • Display nearby physiotherapists on the map (location data is stored only if you choose "Update My Location").
  • Generate PDF session reports and invoices at your request.
  • Improve the App based on aggregated, anonymised usage patterns.
  • Comply with applicable legal obligations.

3. How We Store and Protect Your Data

All data is stored on Supabase (PostgreSQL), hosted on AWS infrastructure in the eu-central-1 (Frankfurt) region by default. Data is encrypted at rest (AES-256) and in transit (TLS 1.2+). Access to health records is enforced by Row-Level Security (RLS) policies — each user can only read and write their own records. Doctors can only access records belonging to their assigned patients.

We do not sell, rent, or share your personal or health data with third parties for marketing purposes.

5. Health Data (Sensitive Personal Information)

PhysioConnect handles health-related information. We treat this data as sensitive personal information and apply the following protections:

  • Health records (SOAP notes, diagnoses, session records) are accessible only to the patient and their explicitly assigned physiotherapist(s).
  • Polyclinic administrators can view aggregate session counts but cannot read individual clinical notes.
  • We do not disclose health data to any third party without your explicit written consent, except where required by applicable law.
  • Clinical notes and SOAP records are never used for advertising, profiling, or any purpose other than direct patient care.
  • Apple Health / Google Fit integration: the App does not read from or write to Apple HealthKit, Google Fit, or any on-device health store.

6. Data Retention and Deletion

We retain your data for as long as your account is active. You may request deletion of your account and all associated data at any time by using the "Delete My Account" option in the App (available in your Profile or Account settings) or by contacting us at the email below. Deletion is permanent and processed within 30 days. Certain records may be retained longer if required by applicable healthcare regulations or for legal compliance; we will inform you of any such retention.

7. Applicable Law and Compliance

We are committed to complying with applicable data protection and health-data laws in the jurisdictions where the App is used. Where the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) or similar regional law applies, you have the rights described in Section 9. If you use the App in a jurisdiction with specific health-data regulations (e.g., HIPAA in the United States), please contact us to discuss data processing agreements before using the App for regulated patient data.

8. Children's Privacy

The App is intended for use by healthcare professionals and adult patients. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13 (or the applicable age of digital consent in your country). If you believe a child has provided us with personal data, please contact us and we will delete it promptly.

9. Your Rights

Depending on your location, you may have the right to:

  • Access – request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • Correction – correct inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Deletion – request that we delete your data ("right to be forgotten").
  • Restriction – object to or restrict certain processing activities.
  • Portability – receive your data in a machine-readable format.
  • Withdraw consent – where processing is based on consent, withdraw it at any time without affecting prior lawful processing.

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at jihadzhour@gmail.com. We will respond within 30 days.

10. Platform-Specific Notices

Apple App Store (iOS / iPadOS)

The iOS version of PhysioConnect uses standard OS-level encryption (TLS/HTTPS) for all network communication. It does not implement proprietary encryption algorithms subject to export-control classification. The App does not use Apple's App Tracking Transparency framework because it contains no cross-app tracking or advertising SDKs.

Google Play (Android)

The Android version uses Firebase Cloud Messaging for push notifications. The FCM token (a device identifier) is sent to our servers solely for notification delivery and is not shared with any other party. Location access is requested at runtime and only when you actively use a location-dependent feature.
